Understanding Your Prescription Before You Buy

Getting an Up-to-Date Eye Exam

Before you order prescription glasses online, you'll need a current prescription from a licensed optometrist or ophthalmologist.

Eye prescriptions typically expire after one to two years, so make sure yours is current.

If you don't have a recent prescription, schedule an eye exam at a local eye doctor.

Many vision insurance plans cover annual eye exams, making this step affordable and essential for your eye health.

When buying eyeglasses online, an accurate prescription is crucial. Even small errors in your prescription details can lead to eye strain, headaches, and blurred vision.

Your prescription should include:

  • Sphere (SPH): Corrects nearsightedness or farsightedness
  • Cylinder (CYL): Corrects astigmatism
  • Axis: Indicates the orientation of astigmatism correction
  • Add: Additional magnifying power for reading (for bifocals/progressives)
  • Pupillary Distance (PD): Distance between your pupils in millimeters

What is Pupillary Distance (PD) and Why It Matters

Your pupillary distance (PD) is the measurement in millimeters between the centers of your pupils.

This critical measurement ensures that the optical center of your lenses aligns perfectly with your eyes, providing optimal vision and comfort.

Most adult PD measurements range from 54mm to 74mm.

When ordering prescription glasses online, accurate PD is essential. An incorrect PD can cause:

  • Eye strain and fatigue
  • Blurred or distorted vision
  • Headaches
  • Difficulty focusing

How to get your PD:

  • Ask your eye doctor during your exam (it may be printed on your prescription)
  • Measure it yourself at home using a ruler and mirror
  • Use FramesDirect.com's printable PD ruler for accurate home measurement
  • Have our opticians help you obtain this measurement

Measuring PD at home: Stand 8 inches from a mirror, hold a millimeter ruler horizontally against your brow, close your right eye and align the ruler's 0mm with the center of your left pupil. Without moving, close your left eye and open your right eye—the mm line that aligns with your right pupil center is your PD.

Understanding Frame Size and Measurements

Proper frame fit is essential when ordering eyeglasses online. All eyeglass frames have measurements (in millimeters) printed on the inside of the temple arm, typically written as three numbers: 52-18-140.

Frame Measurements Explained:

  • Lens Width (52mm): The horizontal width of one lens at its widest point
  • Bridge Width (18mm): Distance between the lenses at the closest point
  • Temple Length (140mm): Length of the arm from hinge to end, including the curved portion

Additional Important Measurements:

  • Frame Width: The total width from hinge to hinge (should align with your face width)
  • Lens Height: Vertical height of the lens (important for bifocals and progressives—at least 30mm recommended)

How to find your perfect size when buying prescription eyeglasses online:

  • Check measurements on your current glasses that fit well
  • Measure your face width from temple to temple
  • Ensure frame width is within 2-4mm of your face width
  • Choose temple length based on head size (135mm-150mm is standard)

Fit Guidelines:

  • Frames should not extend past your temples by more than one finger width
  • Top of frames should align with or sit just above your eyebrows
  • Bottom of frames should not touch your cheeks
  • Frames should not pinch your nose or temples

Step 2 - Select Your Lens Type

Single Vision Lenses

Single vision lenses have one prescription power throughout the entire lens, making them the most common type of prescription glasses. They correct one field of vision—near, intermediate, or distance.

Best for:

  • Nearsightedness (myopia): Difficulty seeing objects far away
  • Farsightedness (hyperopia): Difficulty focusing on close objects
  • Astigmatism: Blurred vision due to irregularly shaped cornea
  • Reading glasses: For close-up tasks only

Benefits of single vision lenses:

  • Simple and easy to adapt to
  • Most affordable lens option
  • Available in all lens materials and coatings
  • Ideal for younger adults and children who need correction at one distance

Single vision lenses are perfect for everyday activities like driving, computer work, or reading. When ordering prescription glasses online with single vision lenses, you'll enjoy clear, consistent vision at your specified distance.

Bifocal Lenses

Bifocal lenses contain two different prescription powers separated by a visible line. The upper portion corrects distance vision, while the lower segment provides magnification for reading and close-up tasks.

Best for:

  • Adults over 40 with presbyopia (age-related near vision difficulty)
  • Anyone needing both distance and near vision correction
  • People who don't want to switch between multiple pairs of glasses

Benefits of bifocals:

  • Convenient two-in-one solution
  • No need to carry separate reading glasses
  • More affordable than progressive lenses
  • Clear visual fields for distance and near vision

Considerations: The visible line may be aesthetically unappealing to some, and there's an abrupt jump between prescriptions that requires adjustment. When buying bifocal glasses online, ensure proper lens height (minimum 30mm) for comfortable viewing.

Progressive No-Line Lenses

Progressive lenses offer the ultimate multifocal solution with a seamless, gradual transition between distance, intermediate, and near vision—all without visible lines. Often called "no-line bifocals," progressives provide a more natural vision experience and youthful appearance.

Best for:

  • Adults with presbyopia who want line-free lenses
  • People who work on computers and need intermediate vision correction
  • Active individuals who need clear vision at all distances
  • Anyone seeking a more aesthetically pleasing multifocal option

Benefits of progressive eyeglasses:

  • No visible lines for a modern look
  • Smooth transition between all viewing distances
  • Ideal for computer work and mid-range tasks
  • Natural vision correction similar to how young eyes focus

Considerations: Progressive lenses require an adaptation period (usually 1-2 weeks) and cost more than single vision or bifocals. They also need precise fitting measurements, including accurate PD and lens height. When ordering progressive glasses online, work closely with certified opticians to ensure proper measurements. Step 4 - Select Optional Lens Treatments and Coatings

Anti-Reflective (AR) Coating - Highly Recommended

Anti-reflective coating (also called anti-glare coating) is one of the most beneficial lens enhancements you can add when ordering prescription glasses online. AR coating eliminates reflections from the front and back surfaces of your lenses, providing multiple benefits:

Benefits of AR coating:

  • Reduces eye strain: Especially during computer use and digital screen time
  • Improves night vision: Reduces glare from headlights and streetlights when driving at night
  • Enhances appearance: Lenses look nearly invisible, allowing better eye contact
  • Increases visual clarity: More light reaches your eyes (up to 99% light transmission)
  • Better for photos: Eliminates reflections from camera flashes
  • Extends lens life: Often paired with scratch-resistant coating for durability

Anti-reflective coating is especially important for high-index lenses, which naturally reflect more light. When buying eyeglasses online, AR coating is strongly recommended for anyone who:

  • Works on computers or digital devices regularly
  • Drives at night
  • Wants the clearest possible vision
  • Prefers a cosmetically appealing, invisible lens appearance

Learn from common pitfalls to ensure a smooth experience when buying prescription glasses online:

  • Forgetting about insurance benefits: Check if you can use FSA/HSA or get vision insurance reimbursement before ordering.
  • Buying without research: Read customer reviews and check frame specifications carefully when shopping for prescription eyeglasses online.

Caring for Your New Prescription Glasses

First-Time Wear and Adjustment Period

When your new prescription glasses arrive, there may be a brief adjustment period:

What to expect:

  • Single vision lenses: Usually immediate comfort with no adjustment needed
  • New prescription strength: 2-3 days to adjust if your prescription has changed
  • Bifocals: 3-5 days to get used to the line and dual viewing zones
  • Progressives: 1-2 weeks to adapt to the gradual power changes (longest adjustment period)

Adjustment tips:

  • Wear your new glasses consistently (don't switch back and forth with old glasses)
  • Move your head more and eyes less when wearing progressives
  • Be patient—your brain needs time to adapt to new vision correction
  • Contact our opticians if discomfort persists beyond the typical adjustment period

When to call for help: If you experience severe headaches, dizziness, or blurred vision after the adjustment period, contact FramesDirect.com's opticians. Sometimes a minor prescription verification or adjustment is needed.

Maintaining Your Eyeglasses

Proper care extends the life of your prescription glasses:

Daily care:

  • Clean lenses daily with lens cleaner spray and microfiber cloth
  • Avoid using paper towels, clothing, or tissues (can scratch lenses)
  • Rinse under lukewarm water before wiping to remove debris
  • Never use household cleaners, ammonia, or vinegar on lenses

Storage:

  • Always store glasses in a protective case when not wearing them
  • Avoid leaving glasses in hot cars (heat can damage lens coatings)
  • Place glasses down with lenses facing up to prevent scratches
  • Keep away from harsh chemicals and cleaning products

Handling:

  • Use both hands to remove glasses (prevents frame warping)
  • Hold frames by the temples, not the lenses
  • Never place glasses on your head (stretches frames)
  • Avoid overtightening screws

When to repair or replace:

  • Loose screws: Can be tightened at home with an eyeglass repair kit
  • Bent frames: Contact customer service for adjustment recommendations
  • Scratched lenses: Deep scratches may require lens replacement
  • Broken frames: Check if frame warranty or protection plan applies

With proper care, your prescription eyeglasses from FramesDirect.com will provide years of clear, comfortable vision.
