What is Pupillary Distance (PD) and Why It Matters

Your pupillary distance (PD) is the measurement in millimeters between the centers of your pupils. This critical measurement ensures that the optical center of your lenses aligns perfectly with your eyes, providing optimal vision and comfort. Most adult PD measurements range from 54mm to 74mm.

When ordering prescription glasses online, accurate PD is essential. An incorrect PD can cause:

Eye strain and fatigue Blurred or distorted vision Headaches Difficulty focusing

How to get your PD:

  • Ask your eye doctor during your exam (it may be printed on your prescription)
  • Measure it yourself at home using a ruler and mirror
  • Use FramesDirect.com's printable PD ruler for accurate home measurement
  • Have our opticians help you obtain this measurement

Measuring PD at home:

Stand 8 inches from a mirror, hold a millimeter ruler horizontally against your brow, close your right eye and align the ruler's 0mm with the center of your left pupil. Without moving, close your left eye and open your right eye—the mm line that aligns with your right pupil center is your PD.

Understanding Frame Size and Measurements

Proper frame fit is essential when ordering eyeglasses online. All eyeglass frames have measurements (in millimeters) printed on the inside of the temple arm, typically written as three numbers: 52-18-140.

Frame Measurements Explained:

Lens Width (52mm): The horizontal width of one lens at its widest point
Bridge Width (18mm): Distance between the lenses at the closest point
Temple Length (140mm): Length of the arm from hinge to end, including the curved portion

Additional Important Measurements:

Frame Width: The total width from hinge to hinge (should align with your face width)
Lens Height: Vertical height of the lens (important for bifocals and progressives—at least 30mm recommended)

How to find your perfect size when buying prescription eyeglasses online:

1

Check measurements on your current glasses that fit well

2

Measure your face width from temple to temple

3

Ensure frame width is within 2-4mm of your face width

4

Choose temple length based on head size (135mm-150mm is standard)

Fit Guidelines:

  • Frames should not extend past your temples by more than one finger width
  • Top of frames should align with or sit just above your eyebrows
  • Bottom of frames should not touch your cheeks
  • Frames should not pinch your nose or temples

Single vision lenses have one prescription power throughout the entire lens, making them the most common type of prescription glasses. They correct one field of vision—near, intermediate, or distance.

Bifocal Lenses

Bifocal lenses contain two different prescription powers separated by a visible line. The upper portion corrects distance vision, while the lower segment provides magnification for reading and close-up tasks.

Best for:

Adults over 40 with presbyopia (age-related near vision difficulty)

Anyone needing both distance and near vision correction

People who don't want to switch between multiple pairs of glasses

Benefits of bifocals:

Convenient two-in-one solution
No need to carry separate reading glasses
More affordable than progressive lenses
Clear visual fields for distance and near vision

Considerations: The visible line may be aesthetically unappealing to some, and there's an abrupt jump between prescriptions that requires adjustment. When buying bifocal glasses online, ensure proper lens height (minimum 30mm) for comfortable viewing.

Progressive No-Line Lenses

Progressive lenses offer the ultimate multifocal solution with a seamless, gradual transition between distance, intermediate, and near vision—all without visible lines. Often called "no-line bifocals," progressives provide a more natural vision experience and youthful appearance.

Best for:

Adults with presbyopia who want line-free lenses

People who work on computers and need intermediate vision correction

Active individuals who need clear vision at all distances

Anyone seeking a more aesthetically pleasing multifocal option

Benefits of progressive eyeglasses:

No visible lines for a modern look
Smooth transition between all viewing distances
Ideal for computer work and mid-range tasks
Natural vision correction similar to how young eyes focus

Considerations: Progressive lenses require an adaptation period (usually 1-2 weeks) and cost more than single vision or bifocals. They also need precise fitting measurements, including accurate PD and lens height. When ordering progressive glasses online, work closely with certified opticians to ensure proper measurements.

Anti-Reflective (AR) Coating - Highly Recommended

Anti-reflective coating (also called anti-glare coating) is one of the most beneficial lens enhancements you can add when ordering prescription glasses online. AR coating eliminates reflections from the front and back surfaces of your lenses, providing multiple benefits:

Benefits of AR coating:

Reduces eye strain: Especially during computer use and digital screen time
Improves night vision: Reduces glare from headlights and streetlights when driving at night
Enhances appearance: Lenses look nearly invisible, allowing better eye contact
Increases visual clarity: More light reaches your eyes (up to 99% light transmission)
Better for photos: Eliminates reflections from camera flashes
Extends lens life: Often paired with scratch-resistant coating for durability

Anti-reflective coating is especially important for high-index lenses, which naturally reflect more light.

When buying eyeglasses online, AR coating is strongly recommended for anyone who:

Works on computers or digital devices regularly
Drives at night
Wants the clearest possible vision
Prefers a cosmetically appealing, invisible lens appearance

Learn from common pitfalls to ensure a smooth experience when buying prescription glasses online.

Using an expired prescription

Always use a current prescription (less than 1-2 years old). Schedule an eye exam if yours has expired.

Incorrect PD measurement

Take your time measuring PD or ask your optician for this measurement. An inaccurate PD causes eye strain and blurred vision.

Choosing frames that don't fit

Use your current glasses' measurements as a starting point and compare to new frames' specifications.

Ignoring lens height for progressives

Progressive and bifocal lenses need adequate lens height (minimum 30mm) to work properly.

Double-check that OD (right eye) and OS (left eye) values are entered correctly.

Skipping important lens options

Choosing the wrong lens material

Forgetting about insurance benefits

Buying without research

Read customer reviews and check frame specifications carefully when shopping for prescription eyeglasses online.
