Best Blue Light Filtering Glasses for Kids

Kids always seem to be on their digital devices, and many displays emit blue light. One thing that many people don’t realize, however, is that computer screens are a very small source of blue light. The majority of blue light exposure comes from sunlight, even inside homes, offices, and schools.

Still, decreasing overall exposure to blue light may improve visual comfort by decreasing glare, especially for kids who spend prolonged periods using digital devices.

Factors to Consider When Choosing Children's Blue Light Glasses

Here are some things to keep in mind if you’re looking for blue light filtering glasses for kids:

  • Lens quality – Opt for high-quality lenses that filter out at least 90% of blue light.
  • Frame durability – Kids can be hard on glasses, so it’s important to get something sturdy. Plastic frames are popular because they’re lightweight, flexible, and durable.
  • Comfort and fit – A comfortable fit is essential to ensure kids actually wear their glasses consistently. Consider bendable frames or a pair with adjustable nose pads and spring hinges for added comfort and flexibility.
  • Additional lens coatings – You may want to add other lens coatings in addition to the blue light filter. An anti-scratch coating can help the lenses last longer and an anti-reflective coating helps reduce glare.

How to Encourage Kids to Wear Blue Light Glasses

Kids’ glasses won’t help if your child won’t wear them. Try these tips to encourage your child to wear their blue light glasses:

  • Let them choose their frame – Once they pick a few favorites, your eye doctor or optician can help make sure the frames are the right fit and size.
  • Take it slow – Start by having your child wear their glasses for 15 minutes while using a device, then gradually increase that time until wearing their glasses becomes a habit. This should also give their eyes time to adjust.
  • Make it cool to wear blue light glasses – Does your child like Harry Potter, Arthur the Aardvark, or Mirabel from Encanto? Remind them that these and many of their other favorite characters wear glasses.
