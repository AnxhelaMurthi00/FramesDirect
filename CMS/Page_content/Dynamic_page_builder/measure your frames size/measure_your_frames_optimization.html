Frame Sizing Guide: Measure Your Eyeglass Frame Size

Finding the perfect fitting pair of eyeglasses online starts with knowing your frame size: eye size (lens width), bridge width, and temple length—all measured in millimeters. These three measurements help ensure comfort and proper fit. Sunglasses often follow the same sizing system but may run slightly larger.

What Do the Frame Size Numbers Mean?

Frame sizes usually appear as three numbers such as 50-18-140 stamped inside the temple arm, bridge, or earpiece. These numbers represent the most important frame measurements.

  • Eye Size: Width of the lens at its widest point (typically 40–62 mm). This is the most important measurement for coverage and fit.
  • Bridge Width: Distance between the lenses that rests across the nose (usually 14–24 mm).
  • Temple Length: Length of the arm from the hinge to the tip behind the ear (commonly 120–150 mm).

A difference of about 2–3 mm from your usual measurements is generally acceptable for a comfortable fit.

How to Find Your Frame Size on Current Glasses

If you already own glasses that fit well, the easiest way to determine your frame size is to check the numbers printed on the inside of the frames.

Check for sizing information on temple or bridge

Eye and bridge measurements are often printed together, while the temple length may appear next to them or slightly separated by model numbers or color codes.

Ignore product codes or model numbers—focus on the two two-digit numbers and one three-digit number representing eye size, bridge width, and temple length.

Typical ranges of measurements • Eye: 40mm–62mm • Bridge: 14mm–24mm • Temple: 120mm–150mm

Frame Size Measurement Chart

  • Eye Size: 40–62 mm – Width of the lens at the widest point. Prioritize this measurement when selecting frames.
  • Bridge Width: 14–24 mm – Distance between lenses that rests on the nose and prevents slipping.
  • Temple Length: 120–150 mm – Length of the arms from hinge to ear tip; should curve comfortably behind the ear.

How to Measure Your Face if You Don't Have Glasses

If you don’t currently have glasses that fit well, you can measure your face using a millimeter ruler or soft measuring tape.

  • Measure horizontally from one side of your face to the other for total frame width.
  • Measure the widest point of the lens area to estimate eye size.
  • Measure the distance between the closest points of the lenses to determine bridge width.
  • Measure from the hinge to the bend behind the ear to estimate temple length.

Sunglasses sizes may run slightly larger, but a tolerance of 2–3 mm still works well for most styles.

Tips for Choosing Frames by Face Size

  • Small: Eye size under 50 mm
  • Medium: Eye size 51–54 mm
  • Large: Eye size 55 mm or larger

Your eyes should appear centered in the lenses. Frames that are too wide or too narrow can make your eyes appear closer together or farther apart.

Practical Advice for the Perfect Fit

  • Use your current glasses as a reference and stay within 2–3 mm of your usual measurements.
  • Choose frames where the temples sit comfortably behind your ears without pressing.
  • If you're unsure about sizing, consult eyewear experts or try virtual fitting tools when available.
