Best Glasses for Your Face Shape: Complete Guide for Every Face

To find the best glasses for your face shape you need to first identify your face shape and find glasses with a shape that will complement your features. With a little knowledge, you’ll be able to find glasses that always enhance your look and never detract from it.

How to Choose Glasses for Your Face Shape

Before choosing the perfect glasses, identify your face shape. Selecting frames that complement your natural features will enhance your look and create balance. Use this guide to find the ideal glasses for your face shape and style.

Best Glasses for Round Faces

Characteristics: Full cheeks, rounded chin, equal length and width.

Frame tips:

  • Choose square or rectangular frames to add contrast.
  • Avoid round frames that exaggerate softness.
  • Bold colors, patterns, and prints can add definition.
  • Clear or thin bridges widen the appearance of the eyes.

Recommended styles: Wayfarer, rectangular, or square frames.

Celebrity examples: Michelle Williams, Aziz Ansari

Best Glasses for Square Faces

Characteristics: Strong jawline, angular features, wide forehead.

Frame tips:

  • Go for round, cat-eye, or oval frames to soften angles.
  • Thin rims in neutral tones make the face appear softer.
  • Avoid thick, square frames that exaggerate angular features.

Recommended styles: Cat-eye glasses, rounded frames.

Celebrity examples: Angelina Jolie, Justin Timberlake

Best Glasses for Oval Faces

Characteristics: Balanced features, high cheekbones, narrow chin.

Frame tips:

  • Almost any frame works, but choose styles slightly wider than the broadest part of the face.
  • Fun colors, bold shapes, and textured frames enhance features.
  • Oversized frames should be balanced carefully.

Recommended styles: Retro cat-eye, square, round, or rectangular frames.

Celebrity examples: Emma Watson, George Clooney

Best Glasses for Heart-Shaped Faces

Characteristics: Broad forehead, pointed chin, high cheekbones.

Frame tips:

  • Frames wider than the forehead help balance proportions.
  • Cat-eye or Clubmaster shapes draw attention away from the forehead.
  • Light colors or detailing soften facial angles.

Recommended styles: Oversized square frames, cat-eye glasses.

Celebrity examples: Halle Berry, Ryan Gosling

Best Glasses for Oblong / Long Faces

Characteristics: Long, slender face, tall forehead, high cheekbones.

Frame tips:

  • Choose tall rims to balance facial length.
  • Wide frames with decorative or color-accented tops add width.
  • Rounded bottom edges soften vertical lines.

Recommended styles: Tall square frames, rectangular frames with accents.

Celebrity examples: Meryl Streep, Will Smith

Best Glasses for Diamond-Shaped Faces

Characteristics: Narrow eye line, broad cheekbones, narrow jawline.

Frame tips:

  • Wide, angular frames complement prominent cheekbones.
  • Top-heavy or semi-rimless styles highlight the eyes.
  • Avoid narrow frames that emphasize the eye line.

Recommended styles: Wide rectangular or cat-eye frames, semi-rimless Clubmaster styles.

Celebrity examples: Megan Fox, Ryan Reynolds

How to Use Face Shape to Guide Glasses Selection

  1. Measure the width of your face across the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline.
  2. Compare your measurements to identify your face shape.
  3. Choose frames that balance your unique features using this guide.

Tips for Choosing Glasses That Fit Your Style

  • Consider lens width and height proportionality.
  • Match frame color to your skin tone, hair color, or outfit.
  • Don’t forget functionality—prescription lenses, comfort, and durability matter.

Frequently Asked Questions About Glasses and Face Shapes

What are the best glasses for my face shape?

The best glasses for your face shape are frames that balance your natural features. Square or rectangular frames add definition to round faces, while round or oval frames soften angular faces. Identifying your face shape first helps narrow down the most flattering frame styles.

How do I determine my face shape for glasses?

You can determine your face shape by measuring the width of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline, and comparing those proportions to common face shape categories such as round, square, oval, heart, diamond, or oblong.

Can I wear any glasses if I have an oval face?

Yes. Oval faces are considered the most versatile face shape. Most frame styles work well, but frames slightly wider than the broadest part of the face tend to offer the best balance.

What glasses should I avoid for my face shape?

In general, avoid frames that closely match your face shape. For example, round frames on round faces or boxy frames on square faces can exaggerate facial features instead of balancing them.

Do face shape rules really matter when choosing glasses?

Face shape guidelines are helpful, but they are not strict rules. Personal style, comfort, prescription needs, and frame size are just as important when choosing the right glasses.

Are sunglasses chosen the same way as eyeglasses?

Yes. The same face shape principles apply to both eyeglasses and sunglasses. However, sunglasses are often larger, so frame proportions should be balanced carefully.

What if my face shape doesn’t fit one category exactly?

Many people have a combination of face shapes. In these cases, choose frames that balance your most prominent features rather than focusing on a single category.
