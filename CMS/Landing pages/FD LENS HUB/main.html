Frequently Asked Questions

What are progressive lenses?

Progressive lenses correct your vision at multiple distances—near, intermediate, and far—all in one seamless lens. Unlike bifocals, there’s no visible line, so you can see clearly without anyone else noticing.

What are transition lenses?

Transition lenses automatically darken outdoors and become clear again indoors. They protect your eyes from UV rays and cut down on glare, making them perfect if you’re constantly moving from inside to outside.

Are contact lenses the same prescription as glasses?

No—your contact lens prescription is usually different from your glasses prescription because contacts sit directly on your eyes. Your doctor will provide you with the correct prescription specifically for contacts.

Are Oakley Prizm lenses polarized?

Some Oakley Prizm™ lenses are polarized, but not all of them. Prizm lenses enhance color and clarity, while polarization reduces glare. Check the product description on our website or contact us if you’re unsure.

What are blue violet light filtering lenses?

Blue violet light filtering lenses help block harmful blue light from digital screens and artificial lighting. They may help reduce eye strain and fatigue if you spend many hours in front of screens every day.

What are high index lenses?

High index lenses are thinner and lighter than regular lenses, ideal for stronger prescriptions. They reduce lens thickness, making your glasses more comfortable and stylish.

Can eyeglasses have non-prescription lenses?

Definitely! Many people choose non-prescription lenses just for style or protection. You can add options like blue-light blocking or anti-reflective coatings, even without prescription.

What is an anti-reflective coating for glasses?

Anti-reflective (AR) coating reduces glare from screens, bright lights, and headlights. It makes your lenses clearer and your eyes more visible, helping you look and see better.
