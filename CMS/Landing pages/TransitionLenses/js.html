Transitions® Light Intelligent Lenses™ FAQs

Do Transitions® Lenses Block UV Rays?

Yes, all Transitions® lenses block 100% of UVA and UVB rays.

Do Transitions® Lenses Filter Blue-Violet Light?

Yes, all Transitions® lenses filter blue-violet light. The degree of protection against blue-violet light when compared to a basic clear lens depends on the Transitions® lens you choose and where you are.

Blue-violet light is between 400 and 455nm as stated by ISO TR20772-2018. (ISO: International Standards Organization – "Ophthalmic optics – Spectacles lenses – Short Wavelength visible solar radiation and the eye, FD ISO/TR 20772")

Can I Wear Transitions® Lenses for Driving?

Yes, but it depends on the kind of Transitions lenses you wear. Transitions® lenses darken because of exposure to UV rays and since the windshield of your car is designed to block UV rays, most Transitions® lenses won’t adequately darken when you’re in a car.

Transitions® XTRActive® new generation lenses are the only Transitions lenses designed to darken when exposed to both UV rays and natural (visible, clear, or white) light so they will work even when you’re in a car.

When Were Transitions® Lenses Invented?

Transitions® were invented in the 1960s; they’re one of the most innovative technologies to emerge in eyeglasses.

Why Should I Register My Transitions® Lenses?

Registering your Transitions lenses assures they are authentic and allows you to participate in their 30-day 'Love Them or Leave Them' risk-free guarantee.

Who Owns Transitions® Lenses?

The Transitions® brand is owned by a joint venture between Essilor (FramesDirect.com’s parent company) and PPG Industries.

Do I Need Anti-Glare Coating With Transitions®?

Transitions® lenses are offered with two types of glare-blocking options, and while neither is needed by everyone, both are preferred by most wearers. Anti-glare, polarized Transitions® lenses are preferred by people who spend time outdoors and/or commuting and need protection from harsh glare caused by reflective surfaces. The anti-reflection coating available with Transitions® lenses is preferred by both those who spend time outdoors, and those who work in an office setting, where vision can be disturbed by reflections caused by lighting, screens, and indoor reflective surfaces.
